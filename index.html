🚲 Bike Theft in NYC

Where bikes disappear — and what we can do about it

About This Project

This project explores NYC bike theft data collected by the NYPD between 2006 and 2024. Using this public dataset, we identify temporal and spatial trends and visualize hotspots across boroughs.

Use the menu below to quickly navigate to different sections of the page: learn about the dataset, explore visualizations, view the interactive theft map, and read our key findings.

Dataset Overview

NYC Open Data from the NYPD with more then 80m theft cases, location, and time info: Visit Dataset .

Time Range

Data spans from 2006 to 2024.

City Coverage

All five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island.

Key Findings

  • Most thefts occur in Manhattan and Brooklyn
  • Summer months have higher incidents
  • Unlocked bikes are especially vulnerable
